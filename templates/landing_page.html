About

In our project on skin lesion classification, we used Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) to improve the interpretability of our machine learning model. Grad-CAM allows us to visualize which areas of an image contribute most to a model's decision, providing a heatmap that highlights the key features driving the classification process. This visualization helps build trust among healthcare professionals and patients alike, as it offers a clear and intuitive explanation of the model's decision-making. By using this approach, we aim to foster confidence in our technology and ensure transparency in our skin lesion diagnosis tool, ultimately contributing to better patient outcomes.


We used a pretrained model, Mobilenet, for it's various benefits, and made it our own by adding new layers to it along with training it using class weights which changed every 30 epochs. The change was dependent on the accuracies of each class, it was done so as to reduce the imbalance in the category wise accuracies therefore not letting a class overpower the others during training.


Motivation



We were motivated to develop a skin lesion classification project using Grad-CAM to address the critical need for early and accurate skin cancer detection. By enhancing model interpretability, we aimed to increase trust and confidence in AI-assisted diagnosis among healthcare providers and patients.
